How to Route Your Audio for a Flawless Demo

To run this 2-sided AI conversation perfectly—without echo, and without your real voice bleeding into the AI's phone call—you must route your audio carefully.

What is BlackHole / VB-Cable?

Because your browser is playing the AI's voice, you need a way to pipe that browser audio directly into the phone call as a "microphone."

  • Mac Users use a free app called BlackHole (2ch).
  • Windows Users use a free app called VB-Audio Virtual Cable (VB-Cable).

Think of these as invisible audio cables inside your computer. They take the sound playing out of your system and plug it directly into the microphone input of your phone app.

Step 1: Create the "Splitter" (Do this first)

Before opening any apps, you must create an audio splitter so that the AI conversation plays out loud on your speakers and gets piped into the phone system.

🍎 For Mac Users:
  1. Open your Mac's Audio MIDI Setup app.
  2. Click the + (bottom left) and select Create Multi-Output Device.
  3. Check both of these boxes in the list:
    • BlackHole 2ch
    • MacBook Pro Speakers (or your external monitor)
🪟 For Windows PC Users:
  1. Install VB-Cable.
  2. Open the Sound Control Panel > Recording tab.
  3. Right-click CABLE Output and select Properties.
  4. Go to the Listen tab, check the "Listen to this device" box, and select your System Speakers from the dropdown. Click Apply.
